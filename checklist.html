The ARO Defensibility Checklist

Twelve checks across four areas. For each, a defensible answer is evidence-based and traceable, not "we think so." If you can't answer three or more with confidence, the cost basis is carrying more uncertainty than the number suggests.

A · Documentation lineage

Traceable basis of estimate

Can you follow the current cost number back to a documented basis, assumptions, quantities, rates, rather than an inherited spreadsheet?

Authorship continuity

Is anyone on the current team able to defend how the estimate was built, or did that knowledge leave with previous operators or staff?

Transfer integrity

Through each change of ownership, was documentation migrated and reconciled, or just handed over as-is?

B · Engineering evidence

Verified structural as-builts

Are primary-steel records confirmed against current condition? Severance points, lift weights and rigging all depend on this.

Current hazmat & NORM inventory

Is there a classified inventory? It drives disposal routing, PPE regime and waste-slot booking, and it's often the biggest surprise.

Isolation philosophy

Do line-status records support a credible make-safe scope, or will the offshore/site campaign discover it the hard way?

Recent inspection data

Is integrity data recent enough to rely on, or are you provisioning against a condition you last saw years ago?

C · Regulatory & waste

Regulator-ready evidence

Could you assemble the evidentiary trail a regulator expects for your end-state, derogation, close-out or full removal, today?

Costed waste pathway

Is the waste strategy tested against real disposal and recycling capacity, with chain-of-custody, not an assumed route?

End-state justification

Is the chosen end-state supported by evidence of net benefit, or is it an assumption nobody has re-tested against current standards?

D · Commercial defensibility

Stated accuracy class

Does the estimate carry an explicit AACE class and range, so the board knows the confidence behind the number, not just the number?

Independent challenge

Has anyone outside the team that built it stress-tested the basis, especially on non-operated positions you fund but don't control?

Counted three or more you couldn't answer with evidence?
