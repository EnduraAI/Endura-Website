01

The problem worth your attention

Why it matters

Australia is carrying $43.6B of decommissioning liability in Commonwealth waters. 58% of it falls due within 15 years. Most of the cost models behind those provisions sit at AACE Class 5: minus 50%, plus 100%. At national scale that is tens of billions of dollars of variance.

The uncertainty has identifiable causes. Documentation fragments across ownership transitions. Integrity degrades late in life while idle well counts grow. Engineering and financial models drift apart. HAZMAT and contamination exposure stays unquantified. And regulatory interpretation keeps tightening.

None of that is fixed by a better spreadsheet. It needs the documentation itself scored, the sensitivity it creates measured, and the remediation work ranked by what it returns. That is what decomm.ai was built to do.

Most ARO models are assumption led. Few are dynamically evidence linked.
decomm.ai platform outline, Rev 0. The gap between those two states is where the variance lives, and where it can be recovered.
FIG. 02 · demo asset · sanitised

Sensitivity, compressed: ±$360M to ±$80M.

On the demo asset, decomm.ai scores documentation across nine domains, runs the cost model through 10,000 Monte Carlo iterations, and ranks remediation by return. Closing the highest-sensitivity gaps moved the band from ±$360M to ±$80M and the estimate from AACE Class 5 toward Class 4.

Demo asset, sanitised. No client data shown. The platform informs the ARO provision basis; the accounting number stays with you and your auditor.
